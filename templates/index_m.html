F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How does DNA barcoding help in identifying species?

    DNA barcoding relies on sequence variation within a short and standardized region of the genome, designated as a “barcode,” to provide accurate species identification. This approach is based on the analysis of the variability within a standard DNA barcode region, which is useful to establish taxonomic relationships.

  • Can DNA be used to distinguish between species?

    Within the mitochondrial DNA, a gene has been widely used to help telling species apart. In most organisms, this gene accumulates mutations just at the right speed so that each species has a unique sequence.

  • How does species identification help in biodiversity conservation?

    Accurate species identification is essential to ecological monitoring. It is used to inform and evaluate conservation actions, such as the monitoring of population trends, the implementation and evaluation of population management plans, health assessments of ecosystems and extinction analysis

  • What can we use to identify species of organisms?

    Identification of organisms to individual scientific names (or codes) may be based on individualistic natural body features, experimentally created individual markers (e.g., color dot patterns), or natural individualistic molecular markers (similar to those used in maternity or paternity identification tests).
